CHANA MASALA
Author: Katie Petrock, via the Internet
Source: The Internet, presumably

3 tablespoons grapeseed oil (substitute with coconut sugar, if needed)
1 medium white or yellow onion
1 tablespoon ground cumin
3/4 teaspoon sea salt (divided)
6 cloves garlic, minced
2 tablespoons fresh ginger, minced
1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
2-3 fresh chilies, sliced, with seeds (Serano pepper, preferred)
1 tablespoon ground corriander
1 teaspoon chili powder
1 teaspoon ground turmeric
1 28oz can finely diced tomatoes
2 15oz cans of chickpeas, slightly drained
1 teaspoon Garam Masala
2-3 tsp coconut sugar
2 tablespoon lemon juice

1. Heat large pot over medium heat. Add oil, onion, cumin, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
2. Add garlic, ginger, cilantro, and chilies to mortar and pestle. Grind into rough paste then add to pan with onions.
3. Add ground coriander, chili powder, and turmeric.
4. Add tomatoes, chickpeas, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. At this point, it should look like a semi-thick soup (add water if needed).
5. Cook at medium high to rolling simmer, then switch to medium low to maintain simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, uncovered.
6. After about 15 to 20 minutes, taste and adjust seasonings to preferrence. (You can add sugar to offset the heat).
7. Remove from heat.  Add lemon juice and Garam Masala.
8. Serve over rice or sweet potatoes and broccoli, or enjoy it plain as a stew.
